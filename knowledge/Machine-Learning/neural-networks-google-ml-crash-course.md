# Neural networks | Machine Learning | Google for Developers

This module introduces neural networks as architectures that capture nonlinear patterns without manual feature crosses. It explains the core components—nodes, hidden layers, and activation functions—and how they interact during inference to produce predictions. The training process is covered at a high level, focusing on backpropagation to optimize parameters and minimize loss. The module also demonstrates approaches for multi-class classification, such as one-vs-all and one-vs-one, and builds on foundational ML topics like regression and data handling.

- Understand why neural networks are useful for nonlinear relationships and reducing manual feature engineering
- Learn the key components of deep neural networks: nodes, hidden layers, activation functions
- Build intuition for the inference pipeline and how activations propagate through layers
- See how backpropagation adjusts weights and biases to minimize loss
- Explore multi-class classification strategies (one-vs-all and one-vs-one)
- Recognize prerequisites: linear/logistic regression, classification metrics, numerical and categorical features, and overfitting/generalization
- Appreciate the contrast with linear models and the role of learned feature crosses
